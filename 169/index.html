    Profits: A Misconceived Concept in Economics

    Summary Notes

    • Profits are often misunderstood as unnecessary charges added to production costs.
    • Socialists like George Bernard Shaw and Karl Marx viewed profits as overcharge or surplus value.
    • Philosopher John Dewey argued that production should prioritize use over profit.

    The Appeal of Socialism

    • Socialism aimed to eliminate supposedly unnecessary charges, making things more affordable for lower-income people.
    • However, when socialism became a real-world economic system, it failed to deliver on its promises:
      • Prices were not lower in socialist countries.
      • The standard of living was not correspondingly higher.

    • Never talk to me about profit; India's first minister, Jawaharlal Nehru, warned country's leading industrialist; SIt is a dirty word{176}
    • Production for use should be subordinated to production for use - John Dewey{177}
